Instruction Manual

A booklet based on Sol LeWitt’s 1968 ‘Projects’ prompt: Not Original.
2026

Image Redaction Manual provides a method of encrypting original artwork or photography. The two encryption methods taught in the manual involve physically rearranging an image to redact the significant details, while also providing instructions on returning the image to its original state. The manual contains step-by-step instructions, visuals, diagrams, and footnotes to guide the user in performing the craft. This project provides a solution for a current and future problem: unauthorized AI scraping of original creations. By rendering the “original” work “not original,” the work can be shared online and avoid potential “not original” reproductions by AI.

Multiple “pocket-sized” copies of the original manual were also produced, making the image redaction process easily accessible and serving as “not original” copies of the project itself.

Image redactions were created manually and converted to digital format to be used as visual content for the manual. Printed manual is 6x9 inches, saddle stitched with transparent acetate pages overlayed on certain signatures.
