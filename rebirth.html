Rebirth

Sculpture Installation.
2023

Rebirth is an installation made from a genuine oak stump, archived newspapers, and wire. The sculpture is a commentary on the lifecycle of natural materials under capitalism, deforestation, and overconsumption. Trees and forests are being harvested for their materials to fill the demand from consumers, yet most of these resources go to waste. While newspapers were once one of the most common uses of paper, the digital age has rendered them almost obsolete. Rebirth serves to remind us how rapidly consumer demands change, but the detrimental effects on the environment remain long after our interest has faded.

The sculpture was made by sourcing a tree stump from a sustainable wood supplier who repurposes unwanted cuts into furniture and more desireable pieces. Wire was sculpted and attached to the stump to form the skeleton, and newspapers were woven in to form the final piece: a ghost of the trees that were used to create it.

This installation was selected and displayed at the TDSB Creates Annual Gallery in 2023.

Tools Used:

  • Oak Tree Stump
  • Chicken Wire, Fishing Wire
  • Newspaper
