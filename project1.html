Topographic Mapping

Graphic Design Research Project Proposal
November 2025

The city of Toronto lacks accessible topographic maps that communicate elevation and terrain for people with visual or mobility issues. This project proposes a new standard mapping system for the city of Toronto. 3D, tactile design methods like raised surfaces, textures, or layered printing would help people better understand the topography of Toronto, communicating elevation and terrain better than 2D or digital systems.

This project consisted of creating 3D models of Toronto's terrain in Adobe Illustrator using real topographic data from the Ontario Government. Streets and transit routes were overlayed onto the 3D map to visualize navigation with the added topographic visuals. A tri-fold brochure acts as the project proposal: introducing the problem, flaws in the current system, and proposes the new standard mapping system with 3D map models.

This project was displayed in the Ada Slaight gallery at OCAD University as part of the Research Methods for Graphic Design exhibit.

Tools Used:

  • Adobe InDesign
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Laser Printer & Bookbinding Materials
